Experience

LJ Walch Company

Following my graduation from Cal Poly, I started as a mechanical engineer at LJ Walch Co in Livermore. I gained experience working on aerospace hardware, reverse engineering components and assemblies to enable cost effective in-house fabrication. Here I further sharpened my skills in SOLIDWORKS and got exposure to aerospace tolerances and failiure modes. I used a Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM) to capture as-built geometry of legacy and repaired components, translating physical parts into accurate CAD representations.

Interface Engineering

During the summer of 2024, I interned on the mechanical engineering team at Interface Engineering in downtown San Francisco for ten weeks. During this time, I got the opportunity to learn CAD software like AutoCAD and Revit, and HVAC software like Trace 700 for heating and cooling load calculations. I was also able to attend various site visits with the team at projects around the Bay Area.

For one small project at UCSF’s Parnassus Heights campus, I personally designed the routing of new ductwork that was to be constructed.

Valvoline Instant Oil Change

During the summer of 2022, I worked at Valvoline Instant Oil Change in San Francisco’s SOMA district. This job allowed me to obtain tons of experience working on cars, which is something that I still enjoy doing.

I also feel that my hands-on experience here helped me to better understand the engineering design of not just cars but all mechanical systems.
